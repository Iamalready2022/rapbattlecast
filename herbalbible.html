Black Seed (Nigella sativa)

Known as "the seed of blessing" in Islamic tradition, praised by Prophet Muhammad as a remedy for every disease except death. Thymoquinone provides bronchodilatory effects, reducing asthma symptoms and improving lung function in studies. Supports immune response against respiratory infections, allergies, and chronic cough. Also aids digestion and liver health.

Dosage/Prep: 1-2g seeds daily or 1 tsp oil; tea or capsules. Cautions: May lower blood sugar/pressure; avoid in pregnancy.

Sources: Sahih Bukhari 7:71:592; PubMed (thymoquinone asthma trials, 2020 meta-analysis).

Frankincense (Boswellia sacra)

Sacred resin from the Bible, used for incense and healing. Boswellic acids inhibit leukotrienes, reducing airway inflammation in asthma and COPD. Inhalation calms nervous system, easing anxiety-related breathing issues. Supports brain health and joint mobility.

Dosage/Prep: 300-500mg extract daily; resin tea or oil diffusion. Cautions: Rare GI upset; consult for autoimmune conditions.

Sources: Exodus 30:34-35; Journal of Ethnopharmacology (Boswellia asthma study, 2019).

Mullein (Verbascum thapsus)

"Lung torch" of folk medicine, used by Hippocrates for pulmonary diseases. Saponins act as expectorants, loosening mucus in bronchitis and whooping cough. Soothes irritated throats and eases smoker's cough. Flowers for ear oil.

Dosage/Prep: 1-2 tsp dried leaf tea, 2-3x daily. Cautions: Strain leaves to remove irritating hairs.

Sources: Hippocrates (400 BC); American Botanical Council (mullein monograph).
